Nutrition

Nowadays there are so many nutrition experts seem to holding opposing opinions and people get easily confused trying to figure out what they should do to optimise their health. So, I am going to share my first-hand experience.

1. Will

I know that it sounds ridiculous, but the most important thing here is your will. First of all, think of your ideal body and make a decision whether you are ready to change.

2. Limit sugar

Unfortunately, findings from several studies point to sugar-sweetened beverages increasing risk of heart disease and type 2 diabetes, even in people who are not carrying excess body fat.

It doesn't mean that you have to stop eating chocolate or drinking soda at all. Do not punish yourself if you ate something, just try to control your eating habits. As a person with 'almost-no-willpower', I know that it's easier said than done but trust me, the most difficult thing is to start.

3. Avoid ultra-processed foods
